Dr. Jose Rizal

One of the greatest heroes of the Philippines

Here's a time line of Dr. Jose Rizal's life:

  • 1961 - Born on June 19 in Calamba, Laguna, Philippines.
  • 1872 - Enters Ateneo Municipal de Manila, a prestigious school, where he excels in academics and obtains a Bachelor of Arts degree with highest honors.
  • 1877 - Enrolls at the University of Santo Tomas to study medicine, but later transfers to the Universidad Central de Madrid in Spain to complete his medical studies.
  • 1882 - Leaves for Spain to continue his education in Europe. Writes numerous essays and articles advocating for Philippine reforms.
  • 1884 - Completes his medical studies at the Universidad Central de Madrid.
  • 1887 - Publishes "Noli Me Tangere," a novel that exposes the abuses of Spanish colonial rule and the clergy in the Philippines.
  • 1889 - Founds La Solidaridad, a newspaper aimed at advocating for political reforms in the Philippines.
  • 1891 - Publishes "El Filibusterismo," a sequel to "Noli Me Tangere," continuing his critique of Spanish colonial rule and advocating for social reforms.
  • 1892 - Returns to the Philippines and establishes the La Liga Filipina, a civic organization aimed at peaceful reform. Arrested and exiled to Dapitan in Mindanao.
  • 1893 - Lives in Dapitan, where he practices medicine, engages in agricultural projects, and teaches local children.
  • 1896 - Accused of sedition and conspiracy by the Spanish authorities. Arrested, tried, and sentenced to death.
  • 1896 - Executed by firing squad on December 30 in Bagumbayan (now Luneta Park) in Manila, becoming a martyr for Philippine independence.

"Rizal's life and legacy are testament to the profound impact that one man's exceptional intellect, unwavering dedication, and visionary ideals can have on the quest for freedom and national progress."

-- Senator Richard Gordon
